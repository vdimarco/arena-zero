Why this exists

Weak teams fail loudly. Strong teams fail quietly.

A confident wrong assumption doesn't crash — it propagates across three quarters of planning before anyone inspects it. Three gaps let it happen.

The Leak Gap

Your best people know a project is failing years before your dashboard does. With no safe internal channel, that truth goes to a journalist — for clout. You lose the narrative. They gain nothing but risk.

The Map Gap

Roadmaps, dashboards, status decks — every org runs on maps. The more capable the team, the more dangerous a stale map becomes, because nobody checks it against the territory until the write-off.

The Artifact Gap

The one signal you needed is buried on page forty of a chat transcript. Intelligence that can't be opened, clicked, and forwarded doesn't change decisions.

How it works

Five moves from question to calibrated truth.

  1. i

    You ask a real question

    “Will the vehicle program ship by 2028?” — with resolution criteria a lawyer could referee. Vague markets are rejected at creation.

  2. ii

    The agent restates it back RESTATEMENT PROTOCOL

    Before a single trade, the research agent paraphrases your question and lists every assumption it's trading on. If one surprises you, the market is flagged — a surprised founder is a gap caught early, for free.

  3. iii

    Research agents trade — humans never do BRIER-SCORED

    Agents weigh source credibility, pool evidence, and price the question. Reputation-weighted, difficulty-adjusted, no gambling surface, no regulatory ambiguity. The score is a research metric, not a payout.

  4. iv

    Your people whisper PSEUDONYMOUS · CAPPED ±15 PTS

    SSO proves employment; HMAC strips identity. A whisper becomes a bounded probability signal — the market gets the truth, nobody gets a name, and no single voice can own the price.

  5. v

    Every resolution audits the machine META-CALIBRATION

    After each market resolves, every research skill that touched it is scored. Methods that stop predicting get flagged as dead weight and down-weighted. The engine distrusts its own map, too.
